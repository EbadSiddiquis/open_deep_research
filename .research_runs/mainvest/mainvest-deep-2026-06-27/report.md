## 1) Executive summary

Mainvest was a **FINRA-registered Regulation Crowdfunding funding portal** (CRD **298384**, SEC File **007-00162**, CIK **0001746059**), founded **2018** in **Salem, MA** by Nick Mathews, Benjamin Blieden and Felix Le Dem (ex-Uber). Its product was the **Revenue Sharing Note (RSN)**: retail investors put in **as little as $100** and received a fixed small percentage of a local brick-and-mortar business's **gross revenue** until they hit a **capped multiple** of principal (across the four non-cannabis exemplars verified — three breweries and one café — caps run ≈**1.1×–1.8×** and are usually **tiered by investor cohort**; treat that as consistent across a small, brewery-weighted sample rather than platform-wide. The cannabis exemplar's headline cap was higher — flat **3.0×** in its original Form C, **amended to a tiered 3.0×/2.0×** in its governing version — see verification run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`), or a balloon at maturity. Over ~6 years it facilitated **~$30–40M** into **450+ businesses** from **~30,000+ investors**, charging issuers **6.0% of the amount raised** and **no fees to investors**.

The instrument and the template were genuinely well-engineered: a standardized, formula-driven Note + Revenue Sharing Agreement + Investment Agreement applied identically across issuers, with the revenue-share rate set on a linear scale **after** the raise closed. The model fit a real, bank-underserved market (restaurants, breweries, cafés), and the revenue-linked structure flexed through COVID.

**It failed for two distinct, separable reasons.** The **proximate cause** was the **May 2024 collapse of Synapse**, the banking-as-a-service middleware Mainvest had outsourced its money movement to. When Synapse's ledger ("dashboard") broke and **Evolve Bank & Trust froze all Synapse activity on ~May 11, 2024**, roughly **$2.4M of Mainvest investor money** — much of it pooled in an in-platform custodial **"Mainvest Wallet"** — was frozen, with residual balances stranded at **Lineage Bank and AMG**. The **root cause**, on the record from a leaked income statement and the CEO's own statements, is that Mainvest **was never profitable** (net loss **>$2.5M in 2021**, **>$2.08M in 2022**), **could not raise capital after 2022**, cut headcount from **~18 to ~5**, and entered 2024 with reportedly **~2.5 months of runway**. A solvent company likely absorbs the Synapse shock; Mainvest could not. Tellingly, competitors **Wefunder and Netcapital did not depend on Synapse and survived**.

The deepest design lesson for an operator: a regulated investment marketplace lives or dies on its **money-movement rails and its servicing continuity**, and Mainvest had concentrated **single-point-of-failure** risk in fragile middleware while running on **thin, never-proven unit economics** (low take rate × high compliance cost × small-ticket, high-touch, long-duration, subordinated/unsecured/non-recourse deals whose **revenue reporting it could not verifiably audit**).

## 2) Findings by dimension

### Dimension 1 — Regulatory structure — **DOCUMENTED** (with two open items)

- **Registration & identifiers — DOCUMENTED.** MainVest, Inc. is a registered funding portal: **CIK 0001746059, SEC File No. 007-00162 (= "7-162"), CRD 298384**, address **81 Washington St, Salem, MA**. The CRD and file number are stated verbatim in every issuer's Form C §N (e.g., the Mantis filing: *"their Central Registration Depository (CRD) number is 298384"*) and the portal appears on FINRA's funding-portal registry ([Mantis Form C §N, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874812/000174605921000363/mantis_offmem.pdf); [Funding Portals We Regulate, FINRA](https://www.finra.org/about/entities-we-regulate/funding-portals-we-regulate)).
- **Registration date — UNRESOLVED.** No primary approval date retrieved; founding year 2018 and the sequential file number are consistent with a 2018 registration, but the exact Form Funding Portal effective date is not pinned.
- **Status anomaly — DOCUMENTED/flag.** As of retrieval (2026-06), Mainvest still appeared on FINRA's *active* registry and was **absent from the "Former Funding Portal Members" list** despite the June 2024 shutdown — i.e., the FINRA membership was seemingly never formally withdrawn (or the registry lags) ([Former Funding Portal Members, FINRA](https://www.finra.org/about/funding-portals-we-regulate/former-funding-portal-members)).
- **What a portal may/may not do — DOCUMENTED.** Under §4(a)(6) / 17 CFR Part 227 Rule 402 and FINRA Funding Portal Rule 300-series, a portal may **not** give advice or recommendations, **solicit** transactions, pay transaction-based comp, or **hold/manage/possess investor funds or securities**. Mainvest's reliance on a third party (Synapse/Evolve) for fund handling is the direct operational consequence of the "cannot hold funds" rule ([17 CFR Part 227, eCFR](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-17/chapter-II/part-227); [Reg CF Compliance Guide for Intermediaries, SEC](https://www.sec.gov/about/divisions-offices/division-trading-markets/division-trading-markets-compliance-guides/regulation-crowdfunding-small-entity-compliance-guide-crowdfunding-intermediaries)). The prohibition is real and enforced — FINRA sanctioned Wefunder's portal for soliciting via >1M emails ([Wefunder AWC, FINRA](https://www.finra.org/sites/default/files/2022-05/Wefunder-2021071940801.pdf)).
- **Per-investor limits — DOCUMENTED.** Pre-3/15/2021: greater of $2,200 or 5% of the *lesser* of income/net worth (if either <$107k); 10% of the lesser (if both ≥$107k); $107k aggregate cap. Post-3/15/2021 amendments removed limits for **accredited** investors and switched non-accredited to the **"greater of"** method; after the 2022 inflation update the threshold became **$124,000** (5%/10% of the greater of income/net worth, capped at $124k) — the version Mainvest displayed at the end ([Crowdfunding Investment Limits, Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/general-resources/news-alerts/alerts-bulletins/investor-bulletins-53)).
- **Issuer cap — DOCUMENTED.** Reg CF aggregate raise cap rose from **$1.07M to $5M** effective **3/15/2021** ([Reg CF cap to $5M, Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2021/03/173206-as-reg-cf-funding-cap-jumps-to-5-million-first-issuers-take-advantage-of-the-increased-funding-amount/)).
- **Ongoing reporting — DOCUMENTED.** Issuer obligations: Form **C** (initial), **C/A** (amendment), **C-U** (progress, within 5 biz days of 50%/100% of target), **C-AR** (annual, within 120 days of FY-end), **C-TR** (termination). These are issuer, not portal, filings ([Reg CF Compliance Guide for Issuers, SEC](https://www.sec.gov/resources-small-businesses/small-business-compliance-guides/regulation-crowdfunding-small-entity-compliance-guide-issuers)).
- **Debt vs. equity positioning — DOCUMENTED.** Mainvest's offerings were **debt** under §4(a)(6); the Mantis primary doc lists `<securityOfferedType>Debt</securityOfferedType>` at `$1.00` face. No evidence Mainvest ran true equity rounds; "Other Classes of Securities" in the Form C are only the founders' LLC interests, *not* offered to investors.

### Dimension 2 — Escrow / payment structure & the Synapse failure — **mixed** (mechanics DOCUMENTED; Mainvest's named escrow agent UNRESOLVED)

- **Reg CF escrow mechanics — DOCUMENTED (verbatim).** A Mainvest Form C states: *"Investor funds will be held in escrow with a qualified third party escrow agent meeting the requirements of Regulation CF ('Escrow Agent') until the Target Offering Amount has been met or exceeded and one or more closings occur"*; all-or-nothing, with a 48-hour cancellation window. **Citation corrected (verification run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`):** this language is in [Mainvest Holdings Corp — Form C, 2022-11-03, exhibit `mainvestformc.pdf` L391–392](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1785840/000178584022000003/mainvestformc.pdf) — **not** the Mantis offering memo, whose §G/J only state that if the target isn't met *"all committed funds will be returned"* ([Mantis Offering Memorandum, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874812/000174605921000363/mantis_offmem.pdf), `mantis_offmem.txt` L220).
- **Banking stack — DOCUMENTED.** **Synapse Financial Technologies** = BaaS middleware; **Evolve Bank & Trust** = partner bank. Mainvest's own closure pop-up: *"Due to the ongoing dispute between Evolve Bank and Synapse, all payment processing on Mainvest is currently unavailable, including withdrawals and bank linkage."* At collapse, residual funds were reportedly at **Lineage Bank and AMG** (Synapse had migrated programs off Evolve pre-failure) ([Funding Portal Mainvest to Cease Operations, Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2024/05/225317-funding-portal-mainvest-to-cease-crowdfunding-operations/); [LCR letter to FINRA/FDIC](https://lawyersforcivilrights.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Mainvest-Collapse-Letter-to-FINRA-and-FDIC-1.pdf)).
- **The "Mainvest Wallet" custodial layer — DOCUMENTED.** Repayments did not flow straight to investor banks; they pooled in an in-platform custodial wallet *"to encourage people to re-invest their repayments"* (LCR letter). This is the FBO/custodial pool that trapped funds — and it sits in tension with the portal "can't hold funds" principle (the holding was via the third-party bank/middleware, which is exactly what broke).
- **Money flow — RECONSTRUCTED.** Investor ACH-in → Synapse-orchestrated FBO/escrow at partner bank → release-on-close to issuer when target met → issuer revenue-share repayments collected → pooled in the Wallet → withdrawable to investor banks. **Plaid** appears as a user-facing bank-link option (see Dim 6) but its exact role in the escrow rail is **UNRESOLVED** ("bank linkage" was a Synapse function).
- **Named escrow agent for Mainvest specifically — UNRESOLVED / conflicting.** Mainvest's own Form C used only generic "qualified third party escrow agent" language. Verbatim proof exists that **Evolve Bank & Trust Co.** was the named Reg CF escrow agent in the *Synapse ecosystem* (e.g., a MicroVentures/Green Valley Form C), and Mainvest's closure notices name Evolve — so Evolve is the **strongest** candidate, but it is not pinned to a verbatim Mainvest contract. One secondary source claims **Fortress Trust LLC**. **Conclusion: candidate set = Evolve (most supported), possibly a Fortress Trust period, residual funds at Lineage + AMG; not lockable to one Mainvest primary contract.**
- **The failure timeline — DOCUMENTED.** **Apr 22, 2024** Synapse files Chapter 11 (C.D. Cal., *In re Synapse Financial Technologies*); **May 8** the TabaPay rescue collapses; **~May 11** Evolve loses access to Synapse's dashboard/ledger and **freezes all Synapse activity** (100k+ users, ~$265M locked industry-wide); **May 17** Boston Business Journal reports Mainvest shutting down; **May 24** Judge Barash appoints ex-FDIC Chair **Jelena McWilliams** as Chapter 11 trustee; **~June 12** Mainvest's withdrawal deadline, then it warns Synapse/Evolve disruptions are blocking those very withdrawals; **June 14** Mainvest ceases operations with **~$2.4M frozen**; **June 24** LCR petitions FINRA/FDIC ([American Prospect, May 23 2024](https://prospect.org/2024/05/23/2024-05-23-fintech-fight-frozen-bank-accounts-synapse/); [Evolve statement, July 12 2024](https://www.getevolved.com/media-release/evolve-bank-trust-statement-on-synapse-bankruptcy/); [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/legal/2025/update-synapse-bankruptcy-trustee-seeks-chapter-7-or-dismissal/)).
- **Ledger of record — DOCUMENTED.** **Synapse** maintained the ledger ("dashboard"); **Evolve** held deposits but, cut off from Synapse's records, could not reconcile which account held what. McWilliams later reported a **$65M–$95M shortfall** across the Synapse estate between bank-held funds and the Synapse ledger ([American Banker](https://www.americanbanker.com/news/synapse-bankruptcy-dismissed-cfpb-may-start-paying-victims)). Mainvest's $2.4M is a small slice inside those Synapse-wide figures — do not conflate.

### Dimension 3 — Deal structure (the Revenue Sharing Note) — **DOCUMENTED (verbatim from real exhibit)**

From the **Mantis Management Group RSN** (Exhibit A) + Revenue Sharing Agreement (Exhibit B), filed 2021-07-26 ([mantis_rsn.pdf, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874812/000174605921000363/mantis_rsn.pdf)):

> ⚠ **Caveat — this exemplar is a cannabis issuer and is NOT a typical deal; the terms below are its *original* filing, since superseded.** Mantis ("Eastcoasterdam") is excluded from federally-regulated banking, and several of its original-filing terms below are **atypical**. A verification pass against four non-cannabis food/beverage issuers (Lucky Goat, WanderLinger, WeHa breweries + Hattie's café — all filed via MainVest) found their notes **ACH-collected and quarterly**, with caps ≈**1.1×–1.8×** usually tiered by cohort — a pattern **consistent across that small, brewery-weighted sample (3 breweries + 1 café), not established as platform-wide**. **The table below is Mantis's *original* Form C; its governing amendments (C/A `…473`, 2021-09-27, and the final note agreement `…007`, 2022-01-04 — both re-pulled and verified) supersede it:** the governing **cap** is a **tiered 3.0×/2.0×** with a **1.0–4.0% revenue scale** (Mantis's cap converged toward the platform's cohort-tiering pattern). Its **payment form did *not* converge**: §3 went manual → ACH (`…473`) → **manual again in the governing `…007`** (see the "Manual…" bullet below), consistent with cannabis ACH/banking exclusion. Rows flagged ⚠ are original-filing anomalies. Full evidence: run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`.

- **Payment math — DOCUMENTED.** Each period: *Purchaser Percentage × Revenue Percentage × Total Revenue.* Payments stop once cumulative payments hit the **Maximum Payment Amount** (Principal × Multiple); a **balloon** trues up any shortfall at maturity (§2.4–2.5); a **Change of Control** accelerates the full remaining Maximum Payment Amount (§2.6).
- **"Total Revenue" (gross revenue) defined — DOCUMENTED.** *"the gross sales price of all merchandise, gift or merchandise certificates, or services sold … using the same method of accounting used for Federal income tax purposes,"* less returns, separately-stated sales taxes, fixed-asset sales, unredeemed gift certificates, discounts/refunds, and retained employee tips (Note §2.7).
- **Non-recourse / no personal or corporate guarantee — DOCUMENTED (resolved by verification run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`).** The Note is solely the obligation of the LLC, and the open "owner-not-liable" item is now pinned to primary text: every non-cannabis modal offering memo states the Notes are *"not secured by any assets of the Company or any assets of persons associated with the Company"* (e.g., [Lucky Goat — Form C](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1792922/000174605919000062/luckygoat_offmo.pdf), `luckygoat_offmo.txt` L354–355; identical at WanderLinger/WeHa/Hattie's), plus *"you will not be personally obligated for any debts of the Company"* (investor-side). No guarantor appears in any exhibit. So **owner non-recourse / no personal-or-corporate guarantee is a filed fact across deals**, not an inference.
- **Pro-rata — DOCUMENTED.** RSA §2: payments pro-rata by principal within an offering; excess "held in trust for the benefit of other Holders."
- **Missed-payment tolerance / default cure — DOCUMENTED.** Event of Default only if non-payment continues **30 days after written notice** (RSA §13.1.1); other covenant breaches get a **90-day** cure (§13.1.6); default interest **1.25%/month (~15%/yr)** (Note §6); force-majeure carve-out (§13.2).
- **Collective-action machinery — DOCUMENTED & notable.** Individual holders **cannot sue**; a single **Representative** (elected by principal-weighted majority, holding a power of attorney) pursues remedies; acceleration needs a majority vote (RSA §13.3–13.4).
- **Manual, non-ACH repayment admin — the cannabis issuer's governing/recurring state; ACH is the non-cannabis standard (corrected by verification run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`; full Mantis amendment chain re-pulled this pass).** All four non-cannabis deals mandate ACH in §3 "Form of Payments" verbatim — *"All payments to Holders will be made as Automated Clearing House (ACH) deposits into an account designated by each Holder at the Site"* (Lucky Goat L526–528; WanderLinger L556–558; WeHa L534–536; Hattie's L500–502) — so **ACH is the standard collection mechanism of the instrument.** Mantis (cannabis) is the anomaly, and its §3/§14.3 *flip-flopped*: **manual** in the base Form C (`mantis_rsn.txt` L532–536, *"fully administered by the Company likely not processed by a financial institution … unlikely … ACH … personal checks and/or third party financial applications"*) → the **standard ACH clause** in C/A `…473` (2021-09-27, [mantis_noteagmtca.pdf](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874812/000174605921000473/mantis_noteagmtca.pdf)) → **reverted to a manual/discretionary clause** in the **final governing note agreement** C/A `…007` (2022-01-04, §3 + §14.3: *"All payments of principal and interest on the Notes will be made in U.S. dollars in such method as the Company may determine. The Company may utilize personal checks and/or third party financial applications…"* [eastcoast_noteagmt2final.pdf](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1874812/000174605922000007/eastcoast_noteagmt2final.pdf)). So Mantis's *governing* collection clause is **manual**, not ACH — the ~3.5-month ACH window (`…473`) was the aberration. This is a **cannabis banking-exclusion artifact** (an issuer cut off from federally-regulated ACH rails couldn't sustain the standard clause), **not a collectibility/servicing property of the instrument**, whose standard remains ACH (4/4 non-cannabis).
- **EaRN Note variant — RECONSTRUCTED.** A two-phase note: Phase 1 pays a revenue % until a cap, then a lower Phase 2 % to maturity, and (unlike the standard RSN) *"will probably not include a maximum amount you can receive"* — i.e., uncapped upside tail ([What is the revenue sharing note?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5096777-what-is-the-revenue-sharing-note)).
- **Who sets terms — DOCUMENTED.** Mainvest **templated** the entire legal stack (identical across Mantis, East Coast, Tu Casa issuers); the **issuer parameterized** headline terms (multiple, maturity, target/max, revenue-% range), and Mainvest computed the final rate post-close.
- **Raise sizing & minimum — DOCUMENTED.** Mantis: **Target $249,000 / Max $250,000**, oversubscription first-come-first-served; **$100 platform-wide minimum** ([Are there minimum investments?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5831742-are-there-minimum-investments)).

### Dimension 4 — Issuer underwriting — **DOCUMENTED (screens); UNRESOLVED (rigor & evidence)**

- **Vetting stack — DOCUMENTED.** Four components: **Responsibility Check** (human phone/email screen for red flags), **Bad Actor Check** (Reg CF Rule 503 limited background check on any **20%+** owner — liens, lawsuits), **Yellow Flag Check** (case-by-case), and **Continuous Review** ([Mainvest Profile, VirtueVest](https://www.virtuevest.com/investments/mainvest); [FinanceBuzz](https://financebuzz.com/mainvest-review)).
- **Social ("community") underwriting — DOCUMENTED.** Issuers had to raise **≥$10,000 from ten people they knew personally** before a public raise — the operational core of the "community underwriting" thesis ([VirtueVest](https://www.virtuevest.com/investments/mainvest)).
- **Financial-statement tiers — DOCUMENTED.** Mainvest applied the **standard Reg CF tiers** (≤$107k self-certified by the principal executive officer; >$107k–<$535k CPA-reviewed; >$535k audited if not the first Title III offering) — i.e., regulatory minimums, not a stricter house standard ([Information the Issuer will Disclose, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5794775-information-the-issuer-will-disclose)). The Mantis filing used the **lowest tier**: *"FINANCIAL INFORMATION NOT INDEPENDENTLY REVIEWED … CERTIFIED BY THE PRINCIPAL EXECUTIVE OFFICER."*
- **"Community underwriting outperforms" thesis — DOCUMENTED as a claim; evidence UNRESOLVED.** Mainvest asserted a portfolio **default rate "75% lower than the 10-year SBA average,"** closure rate **"~1/10th the national average,"** **>96% repayment**, and **~12% cash-on-cash** ([Republic — Mainvest](https://republic.com/mainvest); VirtueVest). **No independent verification, methodology, accept/reject ratio, or risk-grade scale was located** — these are self-reported marketing metrics; whether screening was algorithmic vs. manual is undocumented (it reads as manual). **The >96%-repayment figure, moreover, is not merely uncorroborated — there is an affirmative reason it cannot be checked.** The verification run found **none of the four sampled issuers filed more than one Form C-AR**: each filed a single annual report (Lucky Goat, WanderLinger, WeHa for FY2020; Hattie's for FY2024, then a C-TR eight days later) and then **went dark — lapsing or terminating Reg CF reporting within ~1–2 years**, long before these 5–7-year notes mature (run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`, SQ5; the single-C-AR-then-silence pattern independently confirmed across all four issuers' EDGAR submissions feeds this pass). The public record thus closes before repayment outcomes are observable — the performance stats rest on an internal book that **no surviving disclosure stream can corroborate.**

### Dimension 5 — Disclosure structure — **DOCUMENTED**

- **Form C contents — DOCUMENTED.** The Mantis offering memo is built section-by-section to **17 CFR §227.201 (A)–(Y)**: identity; directors/officers w/ bios; **20%+ owners** (Orphe 50% / Lupo 49%); business & plan; employees; **risk factors**; target & deadline; oversubscription; **use of proceeds** (build-out $234,906 + **Mainvest comp $14,994** = $249,900); cancellation/material-change reconfirmation rights; **terms of securities** (the Note term table, transfer restrictions, dilution); **the funding portal (CRD 298384)**; **portal comp = 6.0% of final offering amount**; indebtedness; prior offerings; **related-party** (none); financial condition; financial statements; bad-actor disqualification; progress updates; annual reports; reporting history.
- **Financials — DOCUMENTED.** CEO-certified, unreviewed; Mantis was pre-revenue (all historical figures $0) with only a management **5-year pro forma** flagged "highly speculative"; the offering invoked SEC **COVID-19 temporary relief**.
- **Investor education & attestations — DOCUMENTED (existence) / RECONSTRUCTED (content).** Mandatory attestations in the Investment Agreement §5 (read the Educational Materials, understand illiquidity/total-loss risk), including the bolded *"YOU SHOULD NOT INVEST ANY FUNDS … UNLESS YOU CAN AFFORD TO LOSE YOUR ENTIRE INVESTMENT."* The live `/educational-materials` page content itself was not retrievable (dead domain).
- **Ongoing disclosure — DOCUMENTED.** Offmem §W commits to a **C-AR within 120 days of FY-end**; Mantis filed a **C-U on 2022-07-12**. Mainvest **standardized** the whole disclosure stack — uniform structure, issuer-specific content confined to bios/plan/use-of-proceeds/pro-forma.

### Dimension 6 — Investor onboarding — **DOCUMENTED**

- Account via Google or email; consent to electronic delivery; **no accreditation required**; self-certified income/net-worth drives the limit calc (post-2022: greater of $2,500 or 5% if either <$124k; 10% if both ≥$124k) ([What is my investment limit?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/3149871-what-is-my-investment-limit)).
- **Bank linking — DOCUMENTED.** Three options: **Plaid** instant login, **voided check**, or manual account/routing with **micro-deposit** verification; **Synapse/Evolve (+Lineage/AMG)** were the back-end custodial rail, distinct from the user-facing Plaid link ([Switching payment methods, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/6155756-switching-payment-methods-to-reinitiate-investments)).
- **Subscription e-sign** via the Investment Agreement; **$100 minimum**; **Reg CF cancellation up to 48h before close** (material change auto-cancels unless reconfirmed in 5 biz days; cancellation lost after close / rolling close) ([How to Invest](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5794769-how-to-invest); [I'd like to cancel](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5096748-i-d-like-to-cancel-my-investment-how-do-i-proceed)). Investor-side KYC/AML specifics are **RECONSTRUCTED** (folded into account creation + bank verification).

### Dimension 7 — Revenue verification (the crux) — **contractually DOCUMENTED (no audit right); operationally UNRESOLVED**

- **DOCUMENTED:** businesses submit a **quarterly gross-revenue report** to their dashboard; Mainvest computes the share and pro-rata payments; no revenue → no payment ([How do repayments work?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5096659-how-do-repayments-work)).
- **Contractually DOCUMENTED, operationally UNRESOLVED (verification run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`).** Read across **five** RSAs (Mantis + the four non-cannabis modal deals), **none contains an audit/inspection right or a revenue-reporting covenant** in the note; each defines **Total Revenue** identically and computes payments off the issuer's *stated* revenue. So *contractually*, **issuer self-report with no verification teeth is the documented norm of the instrument** — not a Mantis quirk and not a gap unique to cannabis. What remains **UNRESOLVED** is the *operational* mechanism: how Mainvest actually collected or checked revenue (POS integration, bank-connection, attestation) is documented in **no** primary filing, and the platform's support center is now defunct. This is still the core design risk for any revenue-share marketplace — but the risk is **structural to the unaudited self-report model**, confirmed across deals.

### Dimension 8 — Repayment servicing — **DOCUMENTED (mechanics); UNRESOLVED (engine, ongoing take-rate)**

- **DOCUMENTED:** quarterly revenue × revenue-% = pool, distributed pro-rata until each investor hits the multiple (worked example: $500k × 5% = $25k that quarter). Repaid funds pooled in the **Mainvest Wallet**. At maturity, unpaid balances → balloon or default. **Mainvest issues no tax forms** — it provides an **Annual Summary** (interest vs. principal) "useful for a 1099-INT," with issuers handling forms inconsistently; no 1099-OID issuance documented ([Tax reporting, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/3178850-information-regarding-tax-reporting-for-mainvest-investors)).
- **RECONSTRUCTED/UNRESOLVED:** the actual ACH/servicing engine and accrual-ledger internals; and whether Mainvest took an **ongoing servicing cut** beyond the ~6% raise fee (no evidence found — treat ongoing take-rate as **UNRESOLVED**). (The standard §3 collection mechanism is **ACH auto-deposit**, on file verbatim at all four non-cannabis issuers; Mantis (cannabis) is the exception — its §3 flip-flopped manual → ACH (`…473`) → **manual again in its final governing note agreement** (`…007`), a banking-exclusion artifact, not the instrument's norm — see Dim 3.)

### Dimension 9 — Collections / default — **DOCUMENTED (process); UNRESOLVED (recovery data)**

- **DOCUMENTED escalation ladder:** one missed cycle → **Default Warning** email + outreach; multiple → **Notice of Default** → **Certified Letter** to investors. On default, **interest accrues**; investors may pursue legal action/bankruptcy, but **Mainvest "is not a collections agency"** and "doesn't assist … whatsoever," though it may help nominate an investor **Representative** ([What happens if a company defaults?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5096656-what-happens-if-a-company-i-ve-invested-in-defaults)).
- **DOCUMENTED:** notes are **subordinated** to bank debt; closed businesses show **"Closed"** status; by **Feb 15** annually Mainvest lists businesses in permanent default and flags any recovery-possible situations; a **loss-declaration** tool existed ([What happens when a business shuts down?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/6122242-what-happens-when-a-business-i-ve-invested-in-shuts-down)).
- **UNRESOLVED:** defined cure-period *durations* beyond the Note's 30/90-day cures; and **any independent default/recovery-rate data** — all favorable numbers are Mainvest's own marketing, and (per Dim 4) the issuers that would evidence recovery stopped filing after a single C-AR, so no public stream tracks it. *(The owner **non-recourse**/no-personal-liability clause, previously flagged unresolved here, is now **DOCUMENTED** — see Dim 3.)*

### Dimension 10 — Investor dashboard — **DOCUMENTED (functions) / RECONSTRUCTED (layout)**

Portfolio view listing each business with revenue/financial performance and repayment status; a detailed **repayment schedule** and an **Annual Summary** (interest vs. principal); document access to each issuer's **Form C** and the Note; a **private per-offering discussion board** (owner + that offering's investors only); the **Mainvest Wallet** for repaid funds; loss-declaration tooling. Exact visual layout, status badges, and feed UI are RECONSTRUCTED (Wayback uncapturable) ([What is Mainvest's role for investors?, Mainvest Support Center](https://support.mainvest.com/en/articles/5096766-what-is-mainvest-s-role-for-investors)).

### Dimension 11 — Business (issuer) dashboard — **DOCUMENTED (anchor) / RECONSTRUCTED (console)**

DOCUMENTED anchor: the **quarterly gross-revenue upload** interface that drives repayment math; document-management onboarding ("examples of required documentation"); investor comms via the discussion board + shared investor contact info. RECONSTRUCTED: campaign-management console (edit offering, target/min/max, rolling-close controls), the remittance interface (how issuers actually funded repayments through the Wallet/Synapse rails), and fundraise analytics.

### Dimension 12 — Marketplace trust layer — **DOCUMENTED**

- **Manufactured trust:** curation/screening as a quality proxy (Responsibility/Bad-Actor/Yellow-Flag/KYC-EIN gating escrow-account opening); transparency via Form C + financials + per-offering Q&A; **social proof** ("over 30,000 investors," "Invest in Main Street," community framing); **track-record display** (>96% repayment, 75%-lower-default claims); a **mission/demographic-reach narrative** (capital for women-, minority-, immigrant-owned, bank-underserved businesses); and **FINRA/SEC-oversight framing**.
- **Trust erosion at the end — DOCUMENTED.** ~$2.4M frozen with "no clear path to recovery," much stranded in the **Wallet**; a **communication whipsaw** (told to withdraw before June 12, then told two days later that Synapse/Evolve disruptions blocked those withdrawals, refunds, investments and repayments); and **orphaned servicing** — businesses left to find new platforms to disburse ongoing repayments, exposed to predatory actors, with no successor-servicer provided ([LCR letter](https://lawyersforcivilrights.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/Mainvest-Collapse-Letter-to-FINRA-and-FDIC-1.pdf)).

### Dimension 13 — Support / compliance ops — **DOCUMENTED (structure); team size CONFLICTING**

- **Support Center** = two hubs (investor + business), topic clusters for onboarding, limits, cancellations, repayments, notes, taxes, shutdown, loss declaration, rolling close ([For Investors collection](https://support.mainvest.com/en/collections/1291988-for-investors)).
- **Compliance workflows:** bad-actor checks, KYC/EIN verification, Form C filing support, and a due-diligence "reasonable basis" review; Form C-AR support implied but not explicitly captured.
- **Team economics — small & CONFLICTING.** Estimates: Republic ~8, Owler ~9, PitchBook ~14, LinkedIn band "11–50"; the company's arc was **~18 (peak, 2019) → ~5 (mid-2023)**. A portal servicing ~30,000 investors and 450+ ongoing long-duration notes on a single-digit/low-double-digit team strongly implies **compliance and servicing did not scale with the book** — the Wallet/Synapse dependency concentrated the risk further.
- **Wind-down comms / FINRA coordination — DOCUMENTED.** Ceased June 14, 2024; said leadership "remains in direct contact with FINRA, Lineage Bank, and AMG" and would assist the trustee — but LCR noted "no information is available on the process" and formally petitioned FINRA (Robert Cook, Katherine Bayer) and FDIC (Chairman Gruenberg) on June 24, 2024.

## 3) Success synthesis (A) — what drove success, ranked by load-bearingness

1. **Product–market fit with bank-underserved Main Street businesses (most load-bearing).** Restaurants, breweries, cafés and dispensaries that banks under-serve found a real capital source; Mainvest funded 450+ of them and **100+ in Massachusetts** (Nightshade Noodle Bar, Mike & Patty's, Top Shelf Cookies, Drawing Board Brewing) ([Boston Globe](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2024/05/31/business/mainvest-salem-crowdfunding-june/)). DOCUMENTED.
2. **A cash-flow-aligned instrument.** The RSN tied repayment to **actual revenue**, not fixed debt service — businesses paid more in good quarters, nothing in dead ones, and pre-revenue startups didn't owe until revenue began (Sharing Start Date). This is why the portfolio reportedly held up through COVID (>95% in good standing a year in, <5% quarterly defaults per Mainvest's 2021 release) ([Mainvest/Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/mainvest-reports-positive-small-business-014500263.html)). DOCUMENTED instrument; performance figures self-reported (and uncheckable to maturity — sampled issuers stopped filing after a single C-AR, see Dim 4 / §5).
3. **$100 minimum + zero investor fees → a broad retail flywheel.** Low ticket and the **community-ownership** pitch drove **~30,000+ investors**, many spreading small amounts across multiple local businesses, with a same-city local-multiplier proof point ("1,051 repayments … from businesses in a city to investors in the same city"). DOCUMENTED.
4. **The community-underwriting thesis as a screening + marketing engine.** The "raise $10k from 10 people you know first" requirement created built-in demand and an accountability signal; the outperformance claims (if taken at face value) were a powerful trust device. DOCUMENTED mechanism / UNVERIFIED claims.
5. **Standardized, templated legal + disclosure stack.** Identical Note/RSA/Investment-Agreement across issuers and a formula-set revenue rate made issuer onboarding scalable and disclosure uniform — a genuine operational asset. DOCUMENTED.
6. **Regulatory tailwind.** The 2016 Reg CF rollout (and the 2021 cap increase to $5M) opened the lane Mainvest rode. DOCUMENTED (2016) / RECONSTRUCTED (2021 as Mainvest-specific tailwind).

### Patterns worth copying (for the operator-reader)
The revenue-share-with-cap instrument, the post-close formula rate, the $100-min retail flywheel, the templated disclosure stack, the "prove local demand first" social-underwriting gate, and the explicit collective-action Representative mechanism are all reusable, well-designed primitives.

## 4) Failure synthesis (B) — proximate vs. root, ranked by causal weight

**Root cause (strongest, most load-bearing): chronic unprofitability + zero capital cushion.** Net losses **>$2.5M (2021)** and **>$2.08M (2022)** (the only hard P&L numbers, from an income statement made public by David Duccini of Silicon Prairie), an **"unsustainable payroll,"** failure to **raise capital after 2022**, headcount cut **~18→5**, and reportedly **~2.5 months of runway** at the end. Mathews conceded 2023 was about profitability and they only "got incredibly close" — i.e., **never profitable after six years** ([Crowdfund Insider](https://www.crowdfundinsider.com/2024/05/225317-funding-portal-mainvest-to-cease-crowdfunding-operations/); [Duccini LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/dvduccini_rip-mainvest-for-the-record-silicon-prairie-activity-7207913251106349057-htwe); [Boston Globe](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2024/05/31/business/mainvest-salem-crowdfunding-june/)). DOCUMENTED.

**The unit-economics machine behind the root cause.** A 6% one-time take on small raises (a $250k raise = ~$15k to Mainvest) against **high fixed compliance cost**, **small-ticket, high-touch deals**, and **long-duration servicing** of subordinated/unsecured/non-recourse notes whose **revenue it couldn't verifiably audit** (no audit right in any RSA — confirmed across five deals, Dim 7) — is a structurally thin model. High investor loss exposure on the instrument also erodes the repeat-capital flywheel over time. DOCUMENTED structure / inferred economics.

**Proximate cause (the precipitant that set the timing): the Synapse/Evolve single point of failure.** Mainvest outsourced money movement to **Synapse**, fragile middleware; the **~May 11, 2024 Evolve freeze** locked ~$2.4M (much in the custodial Wallet) and all payment processing, forcing the June wind-down. Mathews: *"if this Synapse thing hadn't happened, we'd be continuing to operate."* DOCUMENTED.

**The discriminator that separates the two:** **Wefunder and Netcapital did not rely on Synapse and survived** the same collapse ([Boston Globe](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2024/05/31/business/mainvest-salem-crowdfunding-june/)). This confirms Synapse-dependency was a real, Mainvest-specific vulnerability — **and** that those competitors were also better capitalized. So Synapse was the kill-switch; insolvency was the disease.

**Ranked causal weight:**
1. **Root — never reached sustainable unit economics + no capital cushion** (a healthy balance sheet likely absorbs the shock).
2. **Proximate — Synapse/Evolve freeze** (forced the timing; froze $2.4M and all rails).
3. **Structural amplifiers** — outsourced critical rails to fragile middleware vs. direct bank relationships; the custodial **Wallet** concentrating funds; **un-auditable revenue self-reporting** (no audit right in any RSA — structural, confirmed across five deals); **long-duration servicing** of long-tail notes; **compliance/servicing that didn't scale** with the book; and early **COVID concentration** in restaurants/bars (a resilience story, not a death cause).

### Failure modes worth avoiding (for the operator-reader)
Do not single-thread money movement through one BaaS middleware; keep **direct/redundant bank relationships** and a **portable ledger of record**; do not pool customer funds in a custodial wallet you don't control end-to-end; **verify** the data your instrument pays on (don't rely on self-report); plan **servicing continuity / successor-servicer** for long-duration obligations *before* you need it; and don't run a high-compliance, low-take-rate marketplace on perpetual venture subsidy without a real path to unit-economic break-even.

## 5) Conflicting / weak evidence

- **Total deployed: $30M vs. $38M vs. ~$40M.** $30M = Mainvest's own/LCR figure; **$38M** = Boston Globe; "nearly $40M" = the closure statement. Best stated as **~$30–40M** (higher = later/all-time gross). 
- **Businesses: 450+ (all-time) vs. 100+ (Massachusetts-only subset).** Not a contradiction — different denominators. **Investors:** "30,000" vs. "30,000+."
- **Payment frequency (within one filing):** Note term table "Annually" vs. §2.2 titled "Quarterly" vs. offmem "calendar quarter/90 days." The operative legal doc is the Note; Mainvest's marketing described **quarterly** payments — frequency likely varied by deal/era. Flag as a real template inconsistency.
- **Revenue Percentage range:** the platform "1–4%+" scale is RECONSTRUCTED; the Mantis exhibit shows a flat 1.0% (min=max for that small raise). The *mechanic* (linear, post-close, by amount raised) is DOCUMENTED.
- **Escrow agent:** Evolve (strongest) vs. Fortress Trust (one secondary claim) vs. generic in Mainvest's own Form C; residual funds at Lineage + AMG. Not pinned to one Mainvest primary contract.
- **Cause-of-death framing:** Synapse-only (Mathews) vs. pre-existing insolvency (Duccini/financials). Kept distinct above as proximate vs. root.
- **Performance metrics (>96% repayment, 75%-lower-default, ~12% cash-on-cash):** all trace to Mainvest itself; **no independent verification** — treat as marketing, in tension with the post-collapse harm record. **Verification is also structurally foreclosed:** the sampled issuers each filed **at most one C-AR and then went dark** (none >1 annual report), so no public disclosure stream tracks repayment to maturity (see Dim 4; run `mainvest-verify-2026-06-27`).
- **A stray "$100M / 10,000 investors" claim** contradicts every primary source (~30,000 / ~$30–40M) — almost certainly garbled; **do not cite**.
- **Headcount:** 8 / 9 / 14 / "11–50" across aggregators; the **~5–18** arc is consistent with all.
- **Tiered early-vs-later multiple:** now **DOCUMENTED** — absent only from Mantis's *original* Form C (flat 3.0×), but on file in 3 of 4 non-cannabis deals (e.g. WeHa 1.8×/1.5×) and adopted by Mantis itself on amendment (governing C/A: tiered **3.0×/2.0×**, split at first $250k). What remains support-center-only (in no filed exhibit) is **revenue-band rate tiering** and the **EaRN** two-phase percentage. The *prevalence* of cohort tiering is established across a small, brewery-weighted sample, not proven platform-wide.
